Experiment C: Endgame Optimization
====================================
Date: 2026-03-28
Config: 500 games, 2 players, seeds 1000-1499

Hypothesis: Adding endgame awareness (aggressive plays when deck exhausted,
dead card cycling, rank-prioritized plays) will improve scores.

Changes in ruleBotEndgame vs ruleBot:
1. EARLY GAME: Proactively discard dead cards (blocked prerequisites) to cycle deck
2. ENDGAME (turnsLeft !== null):
   - Prioritize plays by rank (lowest first) to build from bottom up
   - On very last turn with 2+ strikes budget: gamble on 50%+ playable cards
   - Skip hints entirely — every turn should be play or discard
3. EARLY GAME: Discard definitely useless before saves (faster cycling)

Baseline (ruleBot):
  Avg: 14.0/25 | Best: 23 | Worst: 4 | Strikes: 1.37
  Distribution: 0-4:1 5-9:30 10-14:240 15-19:223 20-24:6 25:0

Endgame (ruleBotEndgame):
  Avg: 14.1/25 | Best: 23 | Worst: 4 | Strikes: 1.68
  Distribution: 0-4:1 5-9:31 10-14:231 15-19:231 20-24:6 25:0

Delta: +0.1 avg score, +0.31 avg strikes, +8 games in 15-19 range

1000-game validation:
  Base:    Avg 14.0 | Strikes 1.34 | 15-19: 434 | 20-24: 15
  Endgame: Avg 14.1 | Strikes 1.66 | 15-19: 445 | 20-24: 15

Analysis:
- Small positive effect (+0.1 avg). The dead card cycling and endgame
  rank prioritization help marginally.
- Strikes increased (+0.3) due to endgame gambling, but net score still improved.
- The aggressive 50% play threshold was net negative when applied broadly;
  restricting it to only the very last turn with 2+ strikes budget was key.
- Most impact comes from dead card cycling in normal play, not the endgame path.
- The endgame path (turnsLeft !== null) rarely activates meaningfully because
  by that point, most playable cards have already been played or lost.

Conclusion: MARGINAL POSITIVE. The dead card cycling is the most valuable
change. Endgame aggression needs better information (coordinated hints
before deck runs out) to be truly effective — suggests combining with Exp A
(sender-receiver sync) could amplify endgame value.
