## Root Cause Analysis

STEP 1 — Diagnosis (50-game sample with --diagnose):
  All 64 strikes (100%) came from P1-definitelyPlayable.
  Zero strikes from P2-uniqueIdentity or P3+.
  In every case, the possibility matrix had been narrowed to 1 possibility,
  but that possibility was WRONG (e.g., actual=yellow-1, poss=[white-1]).

STEP 2 — Root Cause:
  The bug is in applyNegativeClues() in card-tracker.ts.

  When a color/rank hint is given, applyNegativeClues iterates over the
  player's current hand and marks untouched cards as "not this color/rank".
  However, it uses actionHistory which contains hints from earlier turns.

  After a card is played/discarded and a new card is drawn, the hand's
  card indices SHIFT — cards move to fill the gap and the new card gets
  appended. But applyNegativeClues replays ALL historical hints against
  the CURRENT hand positions, applying old negative information to cards
  that weren't even in the hand when the hint was given.

  This causes aggressive false elimination, narrowing cards to a single
  wrong identity, which then passes isDefinitelyPlayable and causes a strike.

STEP 3 — Fix (ruleBotSafe):
  1. SKIP applyNegativeClues entirely — it's fundamentally broken with
     shifting card indices. Only positive clues (embedded on each card
     object) and visible-card elimination (from buildPossibilities) are
     reliable.
  2. Require at least one positive clue before trusting the matrix for
     play decisions.
  3. Add cross-hand validation for unique identity deductions.
  4. Skip sync play (P3) in safe mode — too risky without validation.

## Implementation

  - New function: ruleBotSafe() in bench-rules.ts
  - CLI flag: --safe to use ruleBotSafe instead of ruleBot
  - CLI flag: --diagnose to log per-strike details
  - Helper functions: countVisible(), verifiedPlayable(), verifiedUniqueIdentity()

## Future Work

  - Fix applyNegativeClues to track card IDs instead of indices, so negative
    clue information survives card draws correctly. This would allow safe
    negative reasoning and potentially even higher scores.
  - The 0-strike result means the bot is now very conservative about playing.
    With a fixed negative clue system, it could play more aggressively while
    remaining safe.
